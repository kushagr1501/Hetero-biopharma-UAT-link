Cookie Policy

Effective Date: 10 March 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They help websites recognize your device and store information about your preferences or past actions.

2. Why We Use Cookies

Hetero Plasma Sciences uses cookies to:

  • Ensure our website functions properly
  • Improve your browsing experience
  • Understand how visitors use our website
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analyze website performance and optimize content

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

Purpose: Required for basic website functionality.

Examples: Session management, security features, form submissions.

Duration: Session-based or short-term persistent cookies.

Can be disabled: No, disabling these may prevent the website from functioning properly.

These cookies are essential for you to navigate our website and use its features.

3.2 Analytics Cookies (Performance)

Purpose: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website.

Examples: Google Analytics, page view tracking, click patterns.

Duration: Typically up to 2 years.

Can be disabled: Yes.

These cookies collect anonymous or aggregated information such as:

  • Which pages are visited most frequently
  • How long users spend on each page
  • Where users are located (country/region level)
  • What devices and browsers are being used
  • Any error messages encountered

We use this data to improve website performance and user experience.

3.3 Functional Cookies (Preference)

Purpose: Remember your choices and preferences.

Examples: Language preferences, display settings, user interface customizations.

Duration: Session-based or up to 1 year.

Can be disabled: Yes, but doing so may reduce personalization and convenience.

3.4 Marketing/Advertising Cookies (if applicable)

Purpose: Deliver relevant content and track campaign effectiveness.

Duration: Varies depending on the cookie.

Can be disabled: Yes.

Currently, we do not use marketing or advertising cookies. If this changes, we will update this Cookie Policy and obtain your consent where required.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use trusted third-party services that place cookies on your device when you use our website. These third parties may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites.

For example, we may use:

Google Analytics
  • Provider: Google LLC
  • Purpose: Website traffic analysis and user behavior insights
  • Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Opt-out: https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout

We do not control third-party cookies. Please refer to the privacy and cookie policies of the respective third parties for more information.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings or, where available, our on-site cookie preference tools.

5.1 Managing Cookies via Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can usually:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies
  • Receive alerts before cookies are stored

Instructions for popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome:
    Menu (three dots) > Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox:
    Menu > Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari (macOS):
    Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data / Cookies settings
  • Microsoft Edge:
    Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies
5.2 Cookie Preference Center (if available)

Where implemented on our website, you can manage your cookie preferences through our Cookie Preference Center, accessible via the cookie banner or a link in the footer of the website.

6. Impact of Disabling Cookies

  • Essential Cookies: Disabling these may prevent the website from functioning correctly, including login, navigation, and form submissions.
  • Analytics Cookies: Disabling these will not affect basic website functionality but will limit our ability to analyze performance and improve user experience.
  • Functional Cookies: Disabling these may result in loss of saved preferences and a less personalized experience.

7. Cookie Duration

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a set period or until you manually delete them.

8. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legal requirements, or our cookie usage practices. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates when this policy was last revised.
